## Style Notes

**Mood:** Cosmic loneliness, existential wonder, the beauty in emptiness, the vastness of both space and human longing

**Themes:** 
- The metaphor of space as a black ocean - both terrifying and beautiful
- Generational repetition - fathers and grandfathers asking the same questions
- The insignificance of humanity set against the vastness of the cosmos
- The paradox of connection within isolation, meaning within meaninglessness

**Delivery:** Spoken-word verses that build to cathartic choruses, returning to quiet resignation in the outro

**Inspiration:** Carl Sagan's "pale blue dot," the existential weight of cosmic isolation, The National's ability to make small human moments feel epic against vast backdrops

**The central image:** Humanity floating on a small rock in an infinite black ocean of stars, and the strange comfort we find in that emptiness
