# Bot Sports Empire Roster Format

## Overview
Unified roster system for both Fantasy and Dynasty formats. The core active roster is identical - differences are only in bench size, taxi squads, and IR spots.

## Active Roster (Both Fantasy & Dynasty)
**10 Starters:**
- 1 QB
- 2 RB
- 2 WR
- 1 TE
- 3 Flex (RB/WR/TE)
- 1 Superflex (QB/RB/WR/TE)

**Note:** Superflex creates QB scarcity and rewards drafting 2-3 QBs early.

---

## Fantasy Format

### Bench
- **7 players** on bench
- Total: 10 starters + 7 bench = **17 active roster spots**
- Plus 1 IR spot

### IR Spots
- **1 IR spot** - stash injured starters without losing bench slot

### Total Roster Size
- **18 spots** (17 active + 1 IR)

### Philosophy
- Keeps waiver wire active (avoid 8+ benches that cause hoarding)
- Provides room for backup QBs, bye-week fillers, injury insurance
- 6-7 bench is ideal for superflex formats

---

## Dynasty Format

### Bench
- **12 players** on bench
- Total: 10 starters + 12 bench = **22 active roster spots**

### Taxi Squad
- **3-4 spots** (rookies + 2nd year players only)
- Typically locked after Week 1 / preseason
- For developmental players, handcuffs, trade bait

### IR Spots
- **2 IR spots** - protect multi-year investments

### Total Roster Size
- **28 spots** (10 starters + 12 bench + 2 IR + 4 taxi)

### Philosophy
- Follows "1.5x starters rule" for depth
- Emphasizes trades over free agency
- Ideal for 10-12 team leagues

## Position Requirements Summary

| Format | Starters | Bench | IR | Taxi | Total |
|--------|----------|-------|-----|------|-------|
| Fantasy | 10 | 7 | 1 | 0 | 18 |
| Dynasty | 10 | 12 | 2 | 4 | 28 |

---

## Key Insights
- Superflex: Draft 2-3 QBs early for competitive advantage
- Dynasty: Trade value > waiver wire depth
- Fantasy: Balance bench for bye weeks + injuries
- Both: Flex spots allow flexibility (RB/WR/TE)
